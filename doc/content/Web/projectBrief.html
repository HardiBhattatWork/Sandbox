Project Brief

Project Summary

The Museum of Comic and Cartoon Art (MoCCA), was founded " to promote the understanding and appreciation of comic and cartoon art as well as to detail and discuss the artistic, cultural, and historical impact of what is the world's most popular art form." To this end the museum offers exhibits, school programs, workshops, lectures, award ceremonies and a yearly Arts Festival.
MoCCA is reconfiguring its gallery space and planning a new line-up of exhibits, programming and events. The redesigned website will highlight the museum's exciting collections, exhibits and activities in an intuitive navigational design and attractive layout that reflect the comic art theme.
The single purpose of the new site is to enhance public awareness of MoCCA and its many services. The secondary purpose of the site is to attract new audiences, increase membership and number of visitors to the museum and expand revenue-generating activities. In the long-term, the site will serve as an integral piece of a major branding campaign that will bring MoCCA to the forefront of comic and cartoon art in the city of New York and more importantly, worldwide.


Audience Profile

The target audience includes everyone interested in the many elements of comic and cartoon art, ranging from storytelling with words and images, to graphic art, to the history and many genres of this art form.
Visitor A: Male. Avid comic book reader/collector. 24 Years old. Frequent visitor of online gaming sites. Decent amount of disposable income. Knows everything about his favorite superheroes, sci-fi and fantasy books. On the site this visitor might search for information about his favorite comic or artist, or browse the collections to discover new books and artists. He may also come to the site for information about museum exhibits and other events, to become a member, or to buy comic art.
Visitor B: Female. Graphic artist/animator. Interested in comics as a form of graphic art and visual storytelling. She accesses the site in search of design inspiration and to learn about lectures or workshops of use to her. She might be interested in becoming a member.
Visitors to the site care about comic art and want to know more about the art form, its history, the artists, and maybe about how to create their own comic art. An advertisement or flyer about a museum exhibit or other event may prompt them to visit the site, as may their desire to learn more about a popular art. When the site visit is a pleasant experience and delivers what they are looking for and more, they are likely to return.


Perception/Tone/Guidelines

The target audience feels that the organization is well respected (the Board contains giants in the industry), organizes exhibits that receive great reviews, and hosts important industry events. However, it feels that the current site does not adequately reflect the organization, the site is disorganized, stale, without coherent design (contains pages in 3 different design styles) and is in need of a makeover.
The target audience should respond to the new online presence with frequent and long visits, positive feedback, becoming a member, numerous purchases and generous donations. Visitors should experience the new site as a beautiful, up-to-date, fun, innovative and informative site about comic art.
The site should visually communicate the theme of comic art in ways that reflect the wide range of expressions in this art. The visual experience should be rich but not overwhelming or confusing. The visual design should reflect the easy access to a wealth of exciting information and activities.
With the new site, the target audience will perceive MoCCA and its site as fun, exciting, beautiful, knowledgeable, informative, trustworthy, innovative and intuitive. Its personality should be perceived as cool and intelligent.


Communication Strategy

To meet our goals - raise public awareness, expand audience and membership - we will enhance the visitor's experience and make the website more consistent with MoCCA's mission to serve as an invaluable resource for a wide audience. We will enhance the visual design, improve on the navigation, provide a sitemap, and restructure the layout. We will facilitate membership registration and, if possible, add search functionality.
The overall message is knowledgeable and fun which will be conveyed throughout the user experience.
We will see the success of redesign when membership and numbers of visitors increase, when there is a wider range of visitors, and when there is an increase in art sales.
We intend to conduct a comprehensive and far-reaching advertising campaign based in the city of New York in sync with the opening of the newly revamped museum. The campaign will include posters in the Metro system buses and trains, comic book shops, book stores and video game stores in the city. For the week prior to the museum reopening, we will have actors dressed up as the different superheroes roaming throughout the city (battling, helping old ladies cross the street, performing heroic feats, etc.)


Competitive Positioning

MoCCA differs from its competition in its physical location in New York City, in the support it receives from a large number of well-known industry veterans, in the yearly Arts Festival it organizes and in the positive exhibit reviews it receives in journals and press. These factors make MoCCA very well positioned to capitalize on the growing interest in comic art from other arts and industries such as filmmaking and gaming, and from ongoing developments in new media.
The new site will build upon MoCCA's reputation and position in the industry of cartoon and comic art with a solid balance of a consistent and aesthetically appealing visual system combined with a simple and intuitive user interface.
