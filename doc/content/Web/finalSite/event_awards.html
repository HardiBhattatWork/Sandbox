Awards

MoCCA Art Festival Awards
Each year the MoCCA Art Festival Award is presented to a creative figure whose work has elevated the cartoon arts. The Award was presented to Jules Feiffer in 2002, and in following years to Art Spiegelman (2003), Roz Chast (2004), Neal Adams (2005), Gahan Wilson (2006), and Alison Bechdel (2007).

This year’s Award recipient, Bill Plympton, is an internationally renowned cartoonist, illustrator, and animator. His graphic novels, include Hair High, Mutant Aliens, Tube Strips, and The Sleazy Cartoons of Bill Plympton. He is best known for his animated films, which include The Tune, I Married a Strange Person, Guard Dog, and Idiots and Angels.

Lulu Awards
The Friends of Lulu, a nonprofit organization that aims to bring more women and girls into the comic book industry, has moved its annual award ceremony from the San Diego Comic-Con to the MoCCA Art Festival. Lulu Awards bring honor and recognition to the most inspiring and noteworthy women in the comic book industry. Past winners include: Rumiko Takahashi, Lynda Barry, Wendy Pini, Marge Henderson Buell, Marie Severin, Dale Messick, Ramona Fradon, Trina Robbins and Hilda Terry.
http://www.friends-lulu.org/awards.php

Harvey Awards
The Harvey Awards, named after pioneering cartoonist Harvey Kurtzman (co-creator of MAD magazine), are one of the comics industry’s oldest and most respected awards. The Awards recognize outstanding achievements in 20 categories, ranging from Best New Talent to the Jack Kirby Hall of Fame. They are the only industry awards both nominated and selected by the full body of comics creating professionals.

Since it beginnings in 1988 the Harveys enjoyed runs at the Dallas Fantasy Fair, Oakland's Wondercon, the Pittsburgh Comicon, and the Museum of Comics and Cartoon Art.
