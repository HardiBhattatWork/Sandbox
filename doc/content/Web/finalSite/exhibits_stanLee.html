Stan Lee: A Retrospective

Feb 23 - Jul 3, 2007

To countless comics fans, Stan Lee is a living legend best known as the major creative force behind the "Marvel Age" of comics. The exhibition addresses his contributions as a writer, artist, art director, editor, promoter, and leader of the "Marvel Revolution" responsible, together with artists Jack Kirby, Steve Ditko, and others for the introduction of a host of novel, complex, humanized, superheroes.

The exhibition includes examples of Lee's early work in comics and addresses his role as co-creator of now world-famous characters such as The Fantastic Four, Spider-Man, The Hulk, Thor, Dr. Strange, Daredevil, and others during the Silver Age of superhero comics.

The exhibit also presents more recent creations such as the animated television show Striperella, and projects at DC Comics, Dark Horse Comics, and POW! Entertainment.
