Toon Town: Comic and Cartoon Art in New York City

Oct 8, 2004 - Apr 6, 2005

This exhibit in MoCCA's newly renovated gallery explores the love affair between The Cartoonists and New York City, and features every aspect of the medium: comic book art, animations, political cartoons, comic strip art, underground comix, superheroes, New Yorker gags, and original cels.

Exhibit highlights include:
Original art from Thomas Nast’s cartoon war on Boss Tweed’s corrupt Democratic machine, which altered the city’s political landscape in the 19th century. Tweed was convicted as a result of the cartoonist’s crusade, but escaped and fled to Spain in 1875. As there were no photos to show Spanish authorities what Tweed looked like, NYPD sent over Nast’s cartoons instead. Tweed was recognized the minute he stepped off the boat and returned to New York for justice.

Animated cartoons created in and lampooning New York, including “A Hare Grows in Manhattan,” with Bugs Bunny as the wascally wabbit, born on the Lower East Side.

The evolution of The New Yorker cartoon with contributions from Liza Donnelly, Roz Chast, Bob Mankoff, Peter de Sève, Barbara Smaller and Mick Stevens.

Comments by the comic and cartoon art community on the tragedy of 9/11 with a series of heartbreaking images.
